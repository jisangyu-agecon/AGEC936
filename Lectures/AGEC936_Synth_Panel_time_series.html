Time-series considerations in Panel data setting

Synthetic control methods

  • Foundational pieces: Abadie, Diamond, and Hainmueller (2010) and Abadie (2019)
  • When?: (Normally but not necessarily), one treated unit (\(i=1\)), many control units, and (somewhat) long time period.
  • Back to potential outcome representation: we want \[ \tau_{t}=y_{1t}(1)-y_{1t}(0) \]
  • What do we observe?
    • \(y_{1t}=y_{1t}(0)\) if \(t<t^*\),
    • \(y_{1t}=y_{1t}(1)\) if \(t>t^*\),
    • \(y_{it}=y_{it}(0)\) for \(i\neq1\).
    • But, we need \(y_{1t}(0)\) for \(t>t^*\)!
  • The key idea: Let’s combine \(y_{it}\) for \(i\neq1\) to estimate \(y_{1t}(0)\) for \(t>t^*\).
  • Underlying model for the potential outcome, \(y_{it}(0)\), is (equation (1) of Abadie, Diamond, and Hainmueller (2010)) \[ y_{it}(0)=\delta_{t}+\theta_t Z_i + \lambda_t\mu_i+\varepsilon_{it}. \]
  • Suppose if there are \(w^*\) that satisfy \[ \sum_{j=2} w^{*}_j y_{js}=y_{1s}\;\;\forall\;\;s<t^*, \] \[ \sum_{j=2} w^{*}_j Z_{j}=Z_{1}\;\;\forall\;\;s<t^*. \]
  • Under certain conditions, we have \(\sum_{j=2} w^{*}_j y_{jt}\) approximately equal to \(y_{1t}(0)\). Thus, we can use \[ \hat{\tau}_t=y_{1t}-\sum_{j=2} w^{*}_j y_{jt}\;\;\forall\;\;t>t^* \]
  • Estimation (see equation (7) of Abadie (2019)): \[ Min_{w_j,j\neq1}\;\left(\sum_h v_h \left(X_{1h}-\sum_{j=2} w_j X_{2j}\right)^2\right) \] subject to \[ w_j\geq 0\;\;\text{and}\;\;\sum_{j=2}w_j = 1 \] and \(X_h\) are “predictors,” which include \(y_{js}\) for \(j\neq 1\) and \(s<t^*\), and \(Z_j\) for \(j\neq1\).
  • How do we choose \(v_h\)?: Abadie, Diamond, and Hainmueller (2010) uses \(v_h\) such that produce \(w_j\) such that they minimize the out-of-sample mean squared prediction error for some set times in the pre-intervention period.
  • Seems cool… any issues?

A simple simulation

clear all
set obs 5
set seed 1234

*id
gen id=_n

*time invariant var
gen x=3*rnormal()

*expand
expand 20

*time var
bys id: gen t=_n

*error
gen e=rnormal()

*treatment to id=1 after t=10
gen d=0
replace d=1 if id==1 & t>=10

*outcome
gen y=2+0.5*x+0.5*t*x+2*t*d+e

*synth
xtset id t
*compare with actual counterfactual
tempfile temp
synth y y(1) y(2) y(3) y(4) y(5) x(1(1)5), trunit(1) trperiod(10) mspeperiod(1(1)7) keep(`temp')

use `temp', clear
gen real_counterfactual=_Y_treated
replace real_counterfactual=_Y_treated-2*_time if _time>=10
label var real_counterfactual "Counterfactual"

*plot
tw (line _Y_treated _time) (line _Y_synthetic _time) (line real_counterfactual _time), graphregion(color(white))
graph export "synth.png", replace

    1. Looks at the relationship between terrorism and regional GDP in Spain.
    2. Specifically, the paper estimates the impact of terrorist conflicts in the Basque Country.
    3. Control units (or potential “donors”) are 16 other Spanish regions.
    4. Sample period: 1955 - 1997 (pre-treatment period: 1955 - 1969, optimization period: 1960 - 1969)
    5. Uses 12 predictors

Synthetic control: Strengths and things to consider (Abadie 2019)

  • See the latest review of Abadie (2019).
  • Strengths
    • No extrapolation: Why matter? See King and Zeng (2006).
    • Sparsity across “donors”: Interpretability
  • Things to consider
    • Effect sizes and the volatility in the outcome of the treated unit.
    • Choosing “predictors” and \(v_h\)
    • Time dimension: the lengths of pre- and post- intervention periods
    • Donor pool
    • Inference
  • Further readings
    • Flexibility versus extrapolation risks
      • Possible negative weights: Doudchenko and Imbens (2016)
      • Non-negative but weights do not necessarily sum to one: Li (2020)
      • Non-negative, weights sum to one, but allow parallel shifts (a constant can be added or subtracted): Goh and Yu (2020)
    • Innovations in inference techniques
      • Frequentist approaches: e.g. Li (2020), Chernozhukov, Wuthrich, and Zhu (2017)
      • Bayesian approaches: e.g. Amjad, Shah, and Shen (2018), Goh and Yu (2020)
    • Extension to disaggregated data: Arkhangelsky et al. (2019), L’Hour and Abadie (2020)
    • De-bias approach: Ben-Michael, Feller, and Rothstein (2018)
